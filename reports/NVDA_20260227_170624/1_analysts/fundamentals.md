## NVIDIA Corporation (NVDA) - Comprehensive Fundamental Analysis Report (as of 2026-02-27)

This report provides a detailed fundamental analysis of NVIDIA Corporation (NVDA), covering its company profile, key financial statements (income statement, balance sheet, and cash flow), and significant financial metrics. The analysis aims to offer insights into the company's financial health and operational performance to inform traders.

### 1. Company Profile and Overview

NVIDIA Corporation operates in the **Technology** sector, specifically within the **Semiconductors** industry. As of February 27, 2026, NVDA boasts a substantial **Market Capitalization of $4,501,516,845,056**. The company's stock has shown significant volatility with a Beta of 2.314, indicating it is considerably more volatile than the broader market. Over the past 52 weeks, the stock has traded between a low of $86.62 and a high of $212.19, with recent averages at $185.40 (50-day) and $174.34 (200-day).

Key valuation metrics include a trailing twelve-month (TTM) PE Ratio of 45.65, which is high, suggesting strong growth expectations. The forward PE of 17.44, however, indicates anticipated earnings growth that could justify the current valuation. The Price to Book ratio stands at a high 28.57, reflecting the market's premium on NVIDIA's assets and future prospects. The company offers a modest Dividend Yield of 0.02%.

### 2. Financial Performance - Income Statement Analysis

NVIDIA has demonstrated exceptional growth in its top and bottom lines over the past few years, particularly in the most recent fiscal year (ending 2025-01-31).

*   **Revenue Growth:** Total Revenue surged dramatically from $26.914 billion in 2022 to an astounding $130.497 billion in 2025. This represents a massive increase, largely driven by demand for its AI and data center products.
*   **Gross Profit:** Gross Profit mirrored revenue growth, climbing from $17.475 billion in 2022 to $97.858 billion in 2025. This indicates strong pricing power and efficient cost management relative to its sales volume. The gross profit margin remains robust.
*   **Operating Income:** Operating Income also saw substantial increases, from $10.041 billion in 2022 to $81.453 billion in 2025. This highlights the company's ability to translate revenue growth into operational profitability. The Operating Margin (TTM) is an impressive 0.65024.
*   **Net Income:** Net Income experienced a similar trajectory, growing from $9.752 billion in 2022 to $72.880 billion in 2025. The Profit Margin (TTM) is a healthy 0.55603.
*   **Earnings Per Share (EPS):** Both Basic and Diluted EPS have seen significant increases. Basic EPS rose from $0.391 in 2022 to $2.97 in 2025, and Diluted EPS from $0.385 to $2.94 over the same period. The TTM EPS is $4.05, with a Forward EPS of $10.59927, signaling continued strong earnings expectations.
*   **Research and Development (R&D):** R&D expenses have consistently increased, from $5.268 billion in 2022 to $12.914 billion in 2025. This sustained investment underscores NVIDIA's commitment to innovation and maintaining its technological leadership.
*   **Tax Provision:** The tax provision has fluctuated but significantly increased in line with higher profits, from $189 million in 2022 to $11.146 billion in 2025.

### 3. Financial Position - Balance Sheet Analysis

NVIDIA's balance sheet reflects a company with rapidly expanding assets and strong equity, though also managing increasing liabilities.

*   **Total Assets:** Total Assets have grown considerably, from $44.187 billion in 2022 to $111.601 billion in 2025, demonstrating significant expansion.
*   **Current Assets:** Current Assets show a robust increase, from $28.829 billion in 2022 to $80.126 billion in 2025. This is largely driven by a substantial rise in Cash, Cash Equivalents, and Short-Term Investments (from $21.208 billion in 2022 to $43.210 billion in 2025) and Receivables (from $4.650 billion in 2022 to $23.065 billion in 2025). Inventory also saw a notable increase.
*   **Total Liabilities:** Total Liabilities have also increased, from $17.575 billion in 2022 to $32.274 billion in 2025, in line with asset growth.
*   **Current Liabilities:** Current Liabilities have risen from $4.335 billion in 2022 to $18.047 billion in 2025, primarily due to increases in Payables and Accrued Expenses.
*   **Total Debt:** Total Debt has fluctuated but remained relatively stable compared to the growth in other areas, standing at $10.270 billion in 2025. Long-term debt has seen a slight decrease from $10.946 billion in 2022 to $8.463 billion in 2025. The Debt to Equity ratio is 7.255, which needs to be considered in context of its high equity.
*   **Stockholders' Equity:** Stockholders' Equity has more than tripled, from $26.612 billion in 2022 to $79.327 billion in 2025, indicating strong retained earnings and financial health.
*   **Working Capital:** Working Capital has significantly improved, moving from $24.494 billion in 2022 to $62.079 billion in 2025, reflecting enhanced short-term liquidity.
*   **Current Ratio:** The Current Ratio is a healthy 3.905, indicating strong ability to cover short-term obligations.
*   **Book Value:** Book Value per share is $6.472, and the overall Tangible Book Value has seen substantial growth.

### 4. Cash Flow Statement Analysis

NVIDIA's cash flow statement showcases robust operational cash generation and strategic capital allocation.

*   **Operating Cash Flow:** Operating Cash Flow has seen an extraordinary increase, from $9.108 billion in 2022 to $64.089 billion in 2025, demonstrating the company's ability to generate significant cash from its core operations.
*   **Investing Cash Flow:** Investing Cash Flow has been negative in most years, indicating significant investments. It was -$9.830 billion in 2022, turned positive briefly in 2023 ($7.375 billion), and then became significantly negative again at -$20.421 billion in 2025. This includes substantial purchases of investments and PPE, reflecting growth-oriented strategies.
*   **Financing Cash Flow:** Financing Cash Flow has been predominantly negative in recent years, largely due to substantial share repurchases and dividend payments. It was $1.865 billion in 2022, but turned negative to -$42.359 billion in 2025, driven by significant repurchases of capital stock ($-33.706 billion in 2025).
*   **Free Cash Flow (FCF):** Free Cash Flow has surged, from $8.132 billion in 2022 to $60.853 billion in 2025 (and TTM FCF is $58.128 billion). This indicates a strong ability to generate cash after accounting for capital expenditures, providing flexibility for strategic initiatives, debt reduction, or shareholder returns.
*   **Capital Expenditures (CapEx):** CapEx has increased from $976 million in 2022 to $3.236 billion in 2025, supporting the company's expansion and technological advancements.
*   **Cash Position:** The company's cash and cash equivalents have steadily increased, with the End Cash Position rising from $1.990 billion in 2022 to $8.589 billion in 2025.

### 5. Key Financial Ratios and Insights

*   **Profitability:** NVIDIA exhibits strong profitability with high profit and operating margins, and impressive Return on Equity (1.01485) and Return on Assets (0.51188) TTM, indicating efficient use of shareholder funds and assets to generate profits.
*   **Liquidity:** The Current Ratio of 3.905 and significantly increasing working capital demonstrate excellent short-term liquidity.
*   **Solvency:** While the Debt to Equity ratio is 7.255, it's important to consider this in context of the massive equity growth and the company's ability to generate substantial cash flows to service its debt. The total debt itself has not seen explosive growth compared to revenue and profit.
*   **Growth:** The company is experiencing hyper-growth across all major financial metrics, particularly revenue, net income, and cash flow from operations. This growth is supported by strategic investments in R&D and CapEx.

### Conclusion and Outlook

NVIDIA Corporation is demonstrating exceptional financial performance, characterized by explosive revenue and earnings growth, robust cash generation, and a strong balance sheet. The company's significant investments in R&D and capital expenditures suggest a continued focus on innovation and market leadership. While valuation metrics like the PE ratio are high, they are arguably supported by the company's remarkable growth trajectory and strong forward earnings estimates. The substantial free cash flow provides ample flexibility for future investments, potential acquisitions, and shareholder returns. The increasing share repurchases indicate management's confidence in the company's intrinsic value.

Traders should note the high volatility (Beta of 2.314) and the premium valuation, which implies that any slowdown in growth or negative news could lead to significant price movements. However, the underlying fundamental strength and market position in critical technologies like AI and data centers present a compelling growth story.

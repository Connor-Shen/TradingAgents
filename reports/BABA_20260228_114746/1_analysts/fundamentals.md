## Comprehensive Fundamental Analysis of Alibaba Group Holding Limited (BABA)

**Date of Analysis: 2026-02-28**

This report provides a detailed analysis of Alibaba Group Holding Limited's (BABA) fundamental information, covering its company profile, financial performance, financial position, and cash flow activities over the past few years, with a focus on recent trends up to fiscal year 2025 and TTM data.

### Company Profile and Basic Financials

Alibaba Group Holding Limited operates in the Consumer Cyclical sector, specifically within the Internet Retail industry, and commands a substantial market capitalization of approximately $344 billion. The company's Beta of 0.39 indicates lower volatility compared to the overall market, suggesting a relatively stable investment for traders.

From a valuation perspective, BABA's trailing twelve months (TTM) Price-to-Earnings (PE) ratio stands at 18.94, while its forward PE ratio is a more attractive 16.31. This suggests that analysts anticipate continued earnings growth, making the stock appear undervalued based on future earnings. The Price-to-Book ratio is 2.21. Earnings Per Share (EPS) for the TTM is $7.61, with a forward EPS projection of $8.84, further reinforcing expectations of future profitability. The company offers a modest dividend yield of 0.73%.

Recent market performance shows positive momentum, with the current price likely trading above both its 50-day ($158.72) and 200-day ($145.03) moving averages, indicating a short-to-medium term uptrend.

Revenue for the TTM period is an impressive $1,012 billion, with a gross profit of $416.7 billion. EBITDA stands at $160.3 billion, and net income for the TTM is $125.4 billion, resulting in a healthy profit margin of approximately 12.19%. However, the operating margin is notably lower at 2.17%, suggesting that a significant portion of profitability might stem from non-operating activities. Return on Equity (ROE) is 11.19%, and Return on Assets (ROA) is 4.03%. The company maintains a manageable Debt to Equity ratio of 27.25% and a current ratio of 1.46, indicating a solid ability to cover short-term liabilities.

A significant concern arises from the TTM Free Cash Flow (FCF), which is reported as a negative $49.5 billion. This starkly contrasts with the positive annual FCF figures and demands further investigation into recent quarterly performance.

### Income Statement Analysis

Alibaba has demonstrated a robust and consistent growth trajectory in its top-line revenue over the last four fiscal years (ending March 31). Total Revenue grew from 853.06 billion in 2022 to 996.35 billion in 2025. This growth has been accompanied by a healthy increase in Gross Profit, which rose from 313.61 billion in 2022 to 398.06 billion in 2025, indicating effective cost of revenue management.

Operating Income has shown strong and consistent growth, increasing from 94.78 billion in 2022 to 147.08 billion in 2025. This highlights the company's ability to generate increasing profits from its core operations. Net Income, including non-controlling interests, experienced a significant surge, more than doubling from 47.08 billion in 2022 to 125.98 billion in 2025. This substantial increase in profitability is also reflected in the Diluted EPS, which climbed from 22.72 in 2022 to 53.60 in 2025.

Pretax Income also followed a strong upward trend, reaching 155.45 billion in 2025. It's important to note the presence of significant non-operating interest income, which contributed to the overall profitability, explaining the difference between operating and net profit margins. The company has consistently reported negative unusual items, primarily due to impairment of capital assets, with the largest impact seen in 2022.

### Balance Sheet Analysis

Alibaba's balance sheet reflects a growing asset base, with Total Assets increasing steadily from 1.70 trillion in 2022 to 1.80 trillion in 2025. However, a crucial trend emerged in 2025 concerning current assets and liquidity. After growing until 2024, Current Assets experienced a notable decrease in 2025, falling from 752.86 billion to 674.05 billion. This decline was primarily driven by a substantial reduction in Cash and Cash Equivalents, which dropped from 248.13 billion in 2024 to 145.49 billion in 2025. This significant decrease in cash holdings and working capital (which also declined sharply in 2025) is a potential red flag for short-term liquidity.

Total Debt has been on a consistent upward trajectory, rising from 176.60 billion in 2022 to 248.11 billion in 2025. While the Debt to Equity ratio remains manageable, the increasing debt load, especially in conjunction with declining cash, warrants close monitoring. Common Stock Equity has generally increased, reflecting shareholder value growth, albeit with a minor dip in 2024.

### Cash Flow Analysis

Operating Cash Flow (OCF) has been strong but shows a declining trend from its peak of 199.75 billion in 2023 to 163.51 billion in 2025. This downward trend in core cash generation is a concern, especially when coupled with increased investment activities.

Investing Cash Flow consistently shows significant outflows. Capital Expenditures surged in 2025 to 85.97 billion, following a period of decrease. Similarly, purchases of investments also saw a large increase in 2025, reaching 136.72 billion. While the company did realize substantial cash from the sale of investments in 2024 (94.05 billion), these sales decreased in 2025 (41.61 billion), leading to a larger net outflow from investing activities in the most recent fiscal year.

Financing Cash Flow has consistently been negative, primarily driven by substantial share repurchases. Alibaba has been actively buying back its shares, with repurchases totaling 86.66 billion in 2025. The company also significantly increased its debt issuance in 2025 to 124.43 billion, alongside higher debt repayments of 59.90 billion.

The annual Free Cash Flow (FCF) for the fiscal year ending March 31, 2025, was positive at 77.54 billion. However, the TTM FCF reported in the fundamental data is a negative 49.49 billion. This critical discrepancy suggests a sharp deterioration in FCF during the quarters subsequent to the March 2025 annual report. This indicates that recent operational or investment activities have consumed a substantial amount of cash, posing a significant concern for traders.

### Key Insights for Traders

*   **Strong Profitability Growth:** Alibaba has demonstrated excellent growth in revenue, gross profit, operating income, and net income, leading to impressive EPS gains. This indicates a fundamentally strong business model.
*   **Deteriorating Short-Term Liquidity:** The substantial decrease in cash and cash equivalents, working capital, and the negative TTM Free Cash Flow are significant concerns. This suggests that while profitability is high, the company's ability to generate and retain cash has weakened recently.
*   **High Investment Activity:** BABA continues to invest heavily in capital expenditures and new ventures. While this can drive future growth, the recent scale of these investments, especially when cash generation is declining, needs careful evaluation.
*   **Aggressive Share Buybacks & Increasing Debt:** Consistent share repurchases indicate management's confidence and can boost EPS, but they are a significant cash drain. The increasing reliance on debt, particularly in a period of negative TTM FCF, could increase financial risk if not managed effectively.
*   **Monitoring Required:** Traders should prioritize monitoring BABA's upcoming quarterly financial reports to understand the drivers behind the negative TTM FCF and the decline in cash.
*   **Valuation vs. Cash Flow:** While valuation metrics like forward P/E appear attractive, the recent cash flow trends present a more cautious picture.

### Conclusion

Alibaba Group Holding Limited presents a mixed financial picture. On one hand, its core business continues to demonstrate strong growth in revenue and profitability, with impressive increases in net income and EPS. On the other hand, recent trends in cash flow and liquidity are concerning, highlighted by a significant drop in cash reserves and a negative TTM Free Cash Flow. The company's aggressive investment strategy and share buybacks, while potentially beneficial long-term, are currently consuming substantial cash. Traders should exercise caution and conduct thorough due diligence on the upcoming quarterly reports to assess the sustainability of its cash generation and liquidity position before making trading decisions.

### 2. Financial Documents Analysis

*   **Operating Income:** Experienced significant growth from $94.78 billion in 2021 to $147.08 billion in 2024, although there was a dip in 2022 ($69.64 billion). The recovery and continued growth in operating income are positive signs.

**Insights:** Alibaba's income statement paints a picture of a company with strong top-line growth and improving profitability. The significant jump in net income and EPS in 2024 is particularly noteworthy. However, the operating margin (2.17% TTM) appears relatively low given the gross profit, suggesting high operating expenses.

#### 2.2. Balance Sheet Analysis (Annual: 2021-2025)

Alibaba's balance sheet reflects a company with substantial assets and a manageable debt structure.

*   **Total Assets:** Have steadily increased from $1,695.55 billion in 2022 to $1,804.23 billion in 2025, demonstrating continuous expansion.
*   **Current Assets:** While generally strong, there was a noticeable decrease from $752.86 billion in 2024 to $674.05 billion in 2025, primarily driven by a significant reduction in "Cash Cash Equivalents And Short Term Investments" from $571.03 billion to $428.09 billion. This warrants attention.
*   **Current Liabilities:** Have also increased, from $383.78 billion in 2022 to $435.35 billion in 2025.
*   **Working Capital:** Although still positive, it has decreased from $331.36 billion in 2024 to $238.70 billion in 2025, mainly due to the reduction in cash and short-term investments.
*   **Total Debt:** Has shown a consistent upward trend, from $176.60 billion in 2022 to $248.11 billion in 2025.
*   **Common Stock Equity:** Has grown steadily from $948.48 billion in 2022 to $1,009.86 billion in 2025, indicating increasing shareholder value.
*   **Debt to Equity:** The TTM ratio is 27.25%, which is relatively healthy for a company of BABA's size and industry, suggesting a reasonable leverage.

**Insights:** While Alibaba's asset base and equity have grown, the decline in current assets, particularly cash, and the increase in total debt in the most recent year (2025) suggest a shift in financial strategy or significant investments. The decrease in working capital should be monitored.

    *   2021: -$198.59 billion
    *   2022: -$135.51 billion

*   **Financing Cash Flow (FCF):** Consistently negative, primarily due to share repurchases and debt repayments.
    *   2021: -$64.45 billion
    *   2022: -$65.62 billion
    *   2023: -$108.24 billion
    *   2024: -$76.22 billion
    *   Notable activities include substantial "Repurchase Of Capital Stock" (e.g., -$86.66 billion in 2024) and "Issuance Of Debt" (e.g., $124.43 billion in 2024) alongside "Repayment Of Debt" (-$59.90 billion in 2024).

*   **Free Cash Flow (Annual):**
    *   2021: $89.44 billion
    *   2022: $165.40 billion
    *   2023: $149.66 billion
    *   2024: $77.54 billion
    *   The TTM Free Cash Flow is -$49.49 billion, a significant negative shift. This is a critical point for traders. The annual FCF has been positive but shows a declining trend from its peak in 2022, culminating in the negative TTM figure. This suggests that the company's operating cash flow is no longer sufficient to cover its capital expenditures and dividend payments/share buybacks, or there have been unusually high investments in the last year.

**Insights:** While operating cash flow remains strong, the substantial negative free cash flow (TTM) is a major concern. The company is investing heavily, evidenced by the large capital expenditures and investment purchases. Aggressive share repurchases, while potentially boosting EPS, contribute to the negative FCF when not fully covered by operating cash. Traders should investigate the nature of these investments and their expected returns.

### 3. Basic Company Financials and Financial History

Alibaba has demonstrated resilience and growth in its core business, as evidenced by consistent revenue and increasing net income. However, its financial strategy appears to be shifting, with increased debt issuance and significant investments.

*   **Growth:** Revenue and net income have shown healthy growth over the observed period.
*   **Profitability:** Profit margins and ROE indicate a profitable enterprise, with recent improvements in net income.
*   **Liquidity:** The current ratio of 1.46 is generally acceptable, but the declining working capital and cash position on the balance sheet, coupled with negative TTM free cash flow, suggest tighter liquidity in the short term if current trends continue.
*   **Leverage:** The Debt to Equity ratio of 27.25% indicates that the company is not overly reliant on debt, though total debt has been increasing.

### 4. Key Insights for Traders

1.  **Strong Core Business & Profitability:** Consistent revenue growth and a significant increase in net income and EPS in 2024 highlight the strength of Alibaba's underlying operations. This could attract growth-oriented investors.
2.  **Aggressive Investment Phase:** The substantial negative TTM Free Cash Flow and high capital expenditures indicate that Alibaba is in an aggressive investment phase. Traders should understand where these investments are directed (e.g., new technologies, market expansion) and their potential for future returns. If these investments are strategic and yield high returns, the current negative FCF could be a temporary dip before future growth.
3.  **Liquidity & Cash Management:** The decrease in current assets, particularly cash, and the declining working capital warrant close monitoring. While the current ratio is adequate, a sustained negative free cash flow could put pressure on liquidity if not managed effectively.
4.  **Shareholder Returns vs. Growth:** The company has been actively repurchasing shares, which can support EPS. However, the combination of share repurchases, increased debt, and significant capital expenditures resulting in negative FCF suggests a balancing act between returning capital to shareholders and funding ambitious growth initiatives.
5.  **Valuation:** A PE ratio of 18.94 and a forward PE of 16.31 suggest that the market expects continued earnings growth. The low Beta (0.39) indicates lower market risk, which might appeal to more conservative investors, despite the operational risks associated with heavy investment.
